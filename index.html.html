Your Entire Claude Cowork

From zero to AI coworker — fast

What you're about to do

Right now, Claude is a chatbot you visit sometimes. After this course, it will be a coworker — one that knows your projects, reads your files, and gets things done without you explaining yourself every single time.

You don't need a tech background. You don't need to know how to code. If you can click a button and create a folder, you can do every step in this course.

Each lesson takes about 2–3 minutes. Do them all at once or one at a time. Either way — let's get you set up.

You will need

A Claude Pro plan ($20/mo) and a Mac or Windows computer. That's it.

You will build

A full AI cowork setup — folder, files, instructions, plugins, and tool connections.

Lesson 1 of 10
Step 1 of 8
Download the App
The one thing you can't skip — and it takes 2 minutes.
⏱ Approx. 2 minutes
What to do
  1. Go to claude.com/download in your browser.
  2. Click the download button that matches your computer — macOS or Windows.
  3. Make sure you have (or upgrade to) a Pro plan at $20/month. You'll need it for Step 2.
  4. Open the app once it's installed. That's Step 1 — done.
Try it now

Open a new browser tab, go to claude.com/download, and download the app right now. Come back when it's installed and open.

Why this matters

The desktop app unlocks the Cowork tab — and that's where the real power lives. The browser version doesn't have it.

Pro tip

Pin the Claude app to your taskbar or dock right now. The people who use it most are the ones who can open it in one click.

Lesson 2 of 10
Step 2 of 8
Click the Cowork Tab
Wrong model = bad output. Right model = a coworker that actually thinks.
⏱ Approx. 1 minute
What to do
  1. Open the Claude desktop app.
  2. At the top, click the Cowork tab.
  3. In the model selector, choose Opus 4.6.
  4. Turn on Extended Thinking.
Try it now

Open the app, click Cowork, set your model to Opus 4.6, and flip on Extended Thinking. Take a screenshot if you want to remember the layout.

Why this matters

Claude has different versions — think regular car vs. sports car. Opus 4.6 with Extended Thinking is the sports car. It reasons through your task before responding, not just grabbing the first answer.

Pro tip

Check your model setting at the start of each session. It resets sometimes — especially if you switch between devices.

Lesson 3 of 10
Step 3 of 8
Build This Exact Folder
This is the step that makes everything else work.
⏱ Approx. 3 minutes
Build this structure
📁 Claude-Work/
📁 ABOUT ME/ ← who you are + how you write
📁 PROJECTS/ ← one subfolder per live project
📁 TEMPLATES/ ← your best past work as structures
📁 OUTPUTS/ ← where Claude saves finished files
Try it now

Create the Claude-Work folder and all four subfolders right now. Name them exactly as shown — spelling and caps matter for consistency.

Why this matters

Cowork has read and write access to this folder. Without it, Claude has no memory of your work between sessions. This is the foundation of everything.

Pro tip

Keep this folder tight. Only put files in here that you actually want Claude to use. Think of it like a desk — cluttered desk, cluttered work.

Lesson 4 of 10
Step 4 of 8
Create 3 Context Files
These files are the secret sauce. They teach Claude who you are.
⏱ Approx. 3 minutes
Your 3 files — inside the ABOUT ME folder
  1. about-me.md — Write what you do day-to-day. Your job, your projects, your goals. A few paragraphs is enough.
  2. my-voice.md — Describe your tone (formal or casual?) and paste 2–3 real samples of your own writing. Emails, posts, anything that sounds like you.
  3. my-rules.md — Your non-negotiables. Ask before starting. Show me a plan. Never delete without my approval.
Try it now

Create all three files inside your ABOUT ME folder. Even rough drafts work — you can update them anytime. Start with my-rules.md first.

Why this matters

These files load automatically every Cowork session. Claude reads them before every task — so it knows your context without you re-explaining every time.

Pro tip

One great file beats 50 random uploads. Put the real stuff in here and Claude will surprise you with how well it knows your style.

Lesson 5 of 10
Step 5 of 8
Set Instructions
Set once. Runs forever. This is how Claude becomes YOUR assistant.
⏱ Approx. 2 minutes
Where to go

Navigate to: Settings → Cowork → Edit Instructions

Paste this — then personalize it
Starter template "I'm [Your Name], [Your Role].
Read my files before every task.
Ask me questions before you execute.
Show me a plan first.
Never delete anything without my approval."
Try it now

Copy the template above, swap in your name and role, and paste it into Settings → Cowork → Edit Instructions. Save it. That's it.

Why this matters

Without standing instructions, Claude starts from scratch every session. With them, it picks up where you left off — every single time.

Pro tip

Update your instructions whenever your role changes or you start a major new project. This is a living document.

Lesson 6 of 10
Step 6 of 8
Stop Prompting.
Start Clicking.
This one changes how you use Claude forever.
⏱ Approx. 2 minutes
Your starter prompt — paste this for every task
Copy this I want to [TASK].
Read all files first.
Ask me questions using AskUserQuestion before you execute.
If something is off, generate new questions.
Do not guess.

When you use this, Claude doesn't just run with your request. It reads your files, then asks clarifying questions as clickable buttons. You click. It executes.

Try it now

Save this starter prompt somewhere easy to grab — a sticky note, phone note, or pinned doc. Then test it: open Cowork, paste it with a real task, and watch what happens.

Why this matters

Vague prompts give vague results. The AskUserQuestion method turns Claude into a collaborator — not a guesser. Your output quality will noticeably jump.

Pro tip

The clickable buttons Claude generates save you from typing long answers. One click beats three paragraphs every time.

Lesson 7 of 10
Step 7 of 8
Install Your First Plugin
This is why $285B shifted out of software stocks.
⏱ Approx. 2 minutes
Where to go

Customize → Browse Plugins → Install

Once installed, type / in any conversation to see your slash commands.

Start with one of these
  1. Marketing → type /marketing:draft-content to generate first drafts of anything marketing-related.
  2. Data → type /data:explore to analyze spreadsheets and uploaded files.
  3. Legal → type /legal to trigger a quick NDA or contract review.
Try it now

Install one plugin — whichever is most relevant to your daily work. Then type / in a new Cowork conversation and explore what's available.

Why this matters

Plugins turn Claude from a generalist into a specialist. When AI can do what software used to do — at no extra cost — the game changes.

Pro tip

Start with just 2 plugins. Too many at once is overwhelming. Add more once you've built the habit of using the first ones.

Lesson 8 of 10
Step 8 of 8
Connect Your Tools
Claude stops being a chatbot. It starts being a coworker.
⏱ Approx. 2 minutes
Where to go

Settings → Connectors → Browse → Add

Popular connectors to start with
  1. Google Drive — Claude reads your docs and references them mid-conversation.
  2. Slack — Claude can pull context from your messages and channels.
  3. Notion — your wikis and notes become available to Claude instantly.
  4. Figma — Claude can reference your designs. Plus 50+ more connectors.
Try it now

Connect the one tool you open every single day. Then test it: ask Claude something that requires it to look at your connected tool. Watch it just work.

Why this matters

Context switching between apps costs you 20+ minutes every day. Connectors eliminate that. No copy-pasting. No tab-switching. Claude lives in your workflow.

Pro tip

Connectors are free on all plans — you don't need a premium tier. Connect one, test it today, then add more as you build the habit.
